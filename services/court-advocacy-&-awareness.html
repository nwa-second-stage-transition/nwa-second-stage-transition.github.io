Court Advocacy & Awareness

Domestic Violence
We help work out marriage or cohabitation problems that may lead to domestic violence. Domestic Violance can come in different forms, including physical, verbal, emotional, economic, religious, reproductive, and sexual abuse, which can range from subtle, coercive forms to marital rape and to violent physical abuse such as choking, beating, female genital mutilation, and acid throwing that results in disfigurement or death.
An Order of Protection
We help you work through the process of obtaining an Order of Protection. An Order of Protection is given by a court to protect a person in a situation involving alleged domestic violence, child abuse, assault, harassment, stalking, or sexual assault.
Restraining Order
Same as an Order of Protection.
No Contact Order
Involve no direct contact such as calling or texting, sending faxes or writing emails, approaching the individual in person, sending gifts, sending messages or posting on social media, and going to a location the individual is known to frequent.
