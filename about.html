About

NWA Second Stage Transition, a nonprofit organization emergency halfway transitional and a continue aftercare recovery living was incorporated in August 2019. A 501 (c) (3) status was approved by the IRS in October 2019. NWA Second Stage shall help encourage transformation to individuals who are willing to break the chain of bondage of abuse in order to find hope and take control of their lives. We seek to strengthen linkages between residents and community-based resources to prevent the progression of abuse, addiction and homelessness.

Mission

NWA Second Stage Transition believes each person is created to be a unique and valuable individual. Our mission is to offer safety and the opportunity for the purpose of change lives. Second Stage Transition will empower and provide a safe environment and supportive home to individuals who want to continue to succeed in an after-care substance abuse recovery, domestics violence and sexual abuse recovery. To provide tools that will help individuals build healthy relationships through transitional living, coaching, growth and education awareness.

Vision

We envision acceptance, empowerment, hope, love, accountability and informed decision making.

Values

Professionalism, Confidentiality, Authenticity, Integrity, Inclusivity , Respect.

Goals and Objectives

  • Will be accepted base on eligibility - a complete full phase of substance abuse treatment from a treatment center.
  • To provide transitional living to residents who will continue to participate in an after-care substance abuse recovery service program to prevent relapse.
  • To provide counseling, art therapy and life skills classes that are designed to reduce the probability of substance abuse, dating violence and sexual assault abuse.
  • Residents will work on financial budgeting for a long term.
  • To help residents recover through engaging in support group, health and wellness classes, life skills program to enhance personal and family skills for employment.
  • To provide an ongoing case management and support system with an effort of the resident's participation-will help them transition into permanent housing upon/after graduation.
  • After graduation, a graduate will be welcome to continue to be part of NWA Second Stage Transition after care outreach services.
